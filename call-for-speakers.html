Speaker Guidelines

Thank you for your interest in speaking at Iterate.PHX!

One of the main goals behind Iterate.PHX is to foster a more-vibrant and connected community, so please let us know about improvements we can make.

Presentation Content

Presentations should try to increase the motiviation and ability of attendees to apply what you are talking-about in their own organizations.

We are trying to help professional software, systems, and operations engineers

  • discover what industry leaders and practictioners do to deliver, deploy, and operate software applications
  • share what works and what doesn't and meet others who want to improve software delivery practices
  • try new frameworks, ideas, and practices

Presentations should try to explain:

  • why the problem being solved is important
  • how the problem was solved using the demonstrated tech with focus-on the general approach to the solution and why it works
    • giving folks enough context to apply the solution to their own environment
    • we are trying to avoid solutions that simplify to "buy commercial service X" or amount to product pitches; however, sessions that explain the journey to commercial service X and the resulting transformation of the organization will be considered
  • unsolved problems and/or opportunities in the space
Time Management

Sessions are scheduled 45 minutes apart, so speakers should plan to keep topic presentations to 40 minutes or less.

Recommended time buckets

  • 30-35 minutes of presentation and demonstration
  • 10-5 minutes of questions & answers
  • 5 minutes of teardown & setup for next session

Please encourage interaction with the attendees throughout the session by, e.g. encouraging questions throughout the presentation and demo.

Topic Ideas

Thinking about hosting a topic session, but not sure what to share? Here are some ideas:


  • Abstract all the Things: Compute, Network, Storage

    Quick tour through the state of the art and how new approaches are changing how we build and operate applications.


  • Culture of DevOps

    What does it mean for a team to have a 'DevOps culture'? What good is it? How do we get one?


  • Mobile Operations: Errors & Performance

    Explain why operating mobile applications is different: latency + power


  • Continuous Delivery of Docker Images

    Approaches and opportunities for building and testing Docker images


  • Continuous Delivery in Highly-Secure Environments

    Case study of CD in a security environment with focus-on challenges and solutions; could broaden motivation if talk was geared-towards how people can introduce security-improving practices to their pipelines


  • Lifecycle of Virtual Machine / System Images

    Survey of how to build and/or spin-up virtual machine instances and configure them dynamically. Compare and contrast mutable vs immutable approaches.


  • Monitoring: what and why to collect

    What raw telemetry should you collect in order to monitor health of system from business and technical perspective


  • System Config Mgmt Intro: Chef vs Puppet vs Ansible vs SaltStack

    Survey of system configuration management leaders comparing a matrix of attributes with “if your team likes x, then start with 1”


  • Clean-up your pipeline using containers

    Show how to use containers to provision clean build environments easily using, e.g. drone.io


  • Measure Front-end Performance in CD

Workshops

Workshop sessions are intended to be practical sessions where leaders and attendees can work together to use a technology or apply a technique. Here are some ideas:


  • Getting started with boot2docker


  • Manage a development environment with docker-compose


  • Diagnose and Improve Front End performance


  • Deploy an ELK cluster to GCE, AWS or locally


  • Build images for multiple targets with Packer


  • Build Docker images for multiple targets with Jenkins
